# Business Story Notes

## Purpose

This file collects commercially useful but still defensible product messaging.

Rule:
- Do not present unverified personal achievements as facts.
- Separate verified facts, product direction, and aspirational brand language.

## Verified Positioning

- Tianye is being built as a hiking-oriented mapping product, not just a generic city map.
- Route geometry is derived from local walkable road data instead of freehand drawing.
- The product direction explicitly treats elevation as a required data dimension for hiking.
- Route time estimation is aligned with the common DAV / DIN 33466 planning rule now used in the product logic.
- The current prototype already supports mobile browser positioning and can recolor the walked portion of a planned route in real time.

## Safe Commercial Language

- Built for real walking scenarios, not just route display.
- Routes stay attached to the underlying walkable network.
- Real-time position tracking helps users compare plan versus actual progress on the trail.
- The roadmap includes elevation-aware effort estimation, ascent/descent analytics, and slope-sensitive route intelligence.
- Planning logic follows a well-known alpine time-estimation convention instead of arbitrary durations.
